About

This page shows the Evolusim evolution simulator. During a simulation a variety of "organisms" gather resources and reproduce. Individual organisms inherit their traits from their parents modified by occasional mutations. An organisms health and traits are partially reflected in its appearance: eye size indicates how far the organism can see; ear size, how far it can hear; tail length, how quickly it can accelerate; eye spacing, its angle of vision. As an organism eats its belly fills up — the white spot in its center shrinks. Having babies costs food. There are other inheritable traits, such as the mutation rate of an organism's genes themselves, that are not reflected in an organism's appearance.

The tabs to the right of this display Google charts representing the state of the simulation over time, except the options tab, which allows you to control the starting state of the simulation.

Things in the Universe

Stones

All animals dislike stones.

Plants

Plants are the foundation of the Evolusim food chain. They obtain a certain amount of energy from the environment with every tick. They are also the only organism that can disperse its babies far away from themselves.

Herbivores

Herbivores and predators can see, hear, move, and react to other things. "Hearing" allows an organism to perceive things in any direction. "Seeing" allows an organism to perceive anything in a wedge in front of it. Sight extends farther than hearing but it only covers an animals front. The larger an animals eyes, the farther it can see. The larger its ears, the farther it can hear. The wider set its eyes, the wider its field of vision.

An animal's maximum velocity is proportional to its radius. The faster an animal can accelerate, the longer its tail.

Carnivores

Carnivores like herbivores and, all else being equal, would rather hang out among the plants.
